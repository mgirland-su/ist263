My Inner Thoughts & Feelings

Red Wine in a Hotel Room

Red wine dripped on to his collar,
Seeping into the sheets and drying in the cracks of her palms,
Decorating the linens like rose petals.

He often finds himself in lonely, crowded hotels
In the rooms of another and another and another
Endlessly creating and destroying and leaving by morning.

But she is alone most nights.
Playing a balancing game of too much yet not enough.
Twisting and reaching and making it to morning.

Some weeks hold hazy days of delicate touches,
Finding fingers to hold on to in messy nights, even if only for a moment.

Because for just a moment, she isn't alone.
Then, she can tell her secrets, whispered between breaths against his lips.

Tonight, he looked up at her as if an angel sat before him,
With her wings strewn to the side.
Every answer he sought out,
Every wandering night looking for the constellations and a sign from God,
Was found between her legs.

Moans echoed throughout the gaudy hotel room,
Or maybe they were screams.
You can't tell when your head's full of laughter
And your stomach's full of butterflies.

Her eyes wandered to the skyline.
Top floor. She knew he would love the view.
A beautiful place for beautiful things to happen before it,
For the details written in the morning must preserve some magic of it all.

He was begging for more,
Or maybe for less.
You can't tell when your hands are a little busy
And you're having this much fun.

One hand closed around his neck
While the other gripped the handle tighter.
Hands messily finding his torso, leaving shaky lines of red welts.
Scratching through his back as if to somehow break through.
Each layer of skin must hold some secret, some answer, beneath.

Past his spine and through his lungs, a gushing, beating heart.

Scraping down his sides,
Tracing each vertebra on the pass upwards,
Kissing collar bones goodbye
For once, she got to be on top.
She got to finish first.

Red wine bloomed through his chest
Down his back and across his pants.
Bottles and bottles saturating the silk sheets,
Running down her bare legs and drying in the cracks of her palms

He would have hated the sight
Would have hated the mess of it all
What was once clean and pristine,
Strategically laundered and prepared for the two,
Was now her final performance.

For the last trick up her sleeve
Or perhaps hidden in the folds of her wings,
Is her final act.
To make a man disappear.
